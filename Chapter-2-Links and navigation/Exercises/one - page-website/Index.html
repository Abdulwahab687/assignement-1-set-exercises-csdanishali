Breaking Bad

Chemistry teacher turned crystal meth producer

Overview

Breaking Bad is an American crime drama television series created and produced by Vince Gilligan. The show aired on AMC from January 20, 2008, to September 29, 2013, consisting of five seasons for a total of 62 episodes.

The series tells the story of Walter White, a struggling and depressed high school chemistry teacher diagnosed with stage-3 lung cancer. Together with his former student Jesse Pinkman, White turns to a life of crime by producing and selling crystallized methamphetamine to secure his family's financial future before he dies.

Main Characters

Walter White

A high school chemistry teacher who is diagnosed with inoperable lung cancer. He turns to manufacturing methamphetamine to secure his family's financial future, partnering with Jesse Pinkman, a former student. As the series progresses, Walter becomes increasingly ruthless.

Jesse Pinkman

A small-time methamphetamine user, manufacturer, and dealer. He becomes Walter's business partner. Despite his involvement in the drug trade, he is far more empathetic than Walt and struggles with the moral consequences of their actions.

Skyler White

Walter's wife who discovers his criminal activities and becomes complicit in his money laundering operations to protect her family.

Hank Schrader

Walter's brother-in-law and a DEA agent who leads the investigation of the meth cooker known as "Heisenberg," unaware that it is Walter.
