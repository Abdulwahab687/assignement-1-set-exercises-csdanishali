Cast & Creative Team

Main Cast

  • Paul Mescal as Lucius Verus

    • The Irish actor, known for his breakout role in "Normal People" and his Oscar-nominated performance in "Aftersun," steps into the sandals of the grown-up Lucius, bringing both physical prowess and emotional depth to the role.

  • Denzel Washington as Marcus Acacius

    • The two-time Academy Award winner brings gravitas and charisma to the role of a former slave turned powerful arms dealer, who becomes a complex mentor figure to Lucius.

  • Pedro Pascal as General Acacius

    • Fresh from his success in "The Last of Us" and "The Mandalorian," Pascal portrays a battle-hardened Roman general whose loyalties remain in question throughout the film.

  • Joseph Quinn as Emperor Geta

    • The "Stranger Things" breakout star plays one of Rome's co-ruling emperors, bringing a nuanced performance as a leader torn between duty and fraternal rivalry.

  • Fred Hechinger as Emperor Caracalla

    • The young actor portrays Geta's more ruthless and ambitious brother, whose hunger for absolute power drives much of the film's political intrigue.

Returning Cast

  • Connie Nielsen as Lucilla

    • Reprising her role from the original film, Nielsen returns as the older and wiser Lucilla, who has survived decades of dangerous imperial politics.

  • Derek Jacobi as Senator Gracchus

    • Another returning cast member from the first film, Jacobi plays an aged but still influential senator who represents the last vestiges of the Roman Republic.

Creative Team

  • Director: Ridley Scott

    • The legendary filmmaker returns to ancient Rome 23 years after the original Gladiator. At 85 years old, Scott brings decades of filmmaking expertise to this ambitious sequel.

  • Screenplay: David Scarpa

    • The writer who previously collaborated with Scott on "All the Money in the World" crafts a story that honors the original while carving new territory.

  • Producers: Ridley Scott, Michael Pruss, Douglas Wick, Lucy Fisher

    • Wick and Fisher also produced the original film, bringing continuity to the franchise.

  • Cinematography: John Mathieson

    • The original film's cinematographer returns, maintaining visual continuity while embracing new technologies.

  • Music: Harry Gregson-Williams

    • Taking over from the late Hans Zimmer, Gregson-Williams incorporates themes from the original score while creating a distinctive new musical identity.

  • Production Companies: Paramount Pictures, Scott Free Productions, Red Wagon Entertainment
