Chicken Biryani

A majestic one-pot dish of basmati rice layered with tender marinated chicken, caramelized onions, and fragrant spices. This classic Indian biryani combines bold flavors and delicate textures for a truly memorable feast that's perfect for special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 750g chicken pieces, bone-in
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 3 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • ¼ cup chopped mint leaves
  • ¼ cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 star anise
  • A pinch of saffron soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water for rice
  • Lemon wedges for serving
  • Raita (yogurt side dish) for serving

Method

  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, half of the ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Keep aside for at least 1 hour, preferably longer.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of ghee in a large heavy-bottomed pan. Fry the sliced onions until golden brown and caramelized. Remove half and set aside for layering.
  3. In the same pan with remaining onions, add the whole spices (cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, bay leaves, star anise) and sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add the remaining ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add the marinated chicken, chopped tomatoes, coriander powder, cumin powder, and half of the mint and coriander leaves. Cook for 10-15 minutes until the chicken is partially cooked and oil separates from the masala.
  6. In a separate large pot, bring water to a boil with salt. Add the soaked and drained rice and cook until 70% done (about 5-7 minutes). Drain the rice.
  7. Now for layering: spread half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture. Sprinkle half of the remaining mint and coriander leaves, some fried onions, and half of the saffron milk.
  8. Add the remaining rice on top, followed by the rest of the fried onions, herbs, and saffron milk. Drizzle the remaining ghee on top.
  9. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid (or use dough to seal the edges). Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the rice to fully cook.
  10. Let it rest for 10 minutes after turning off the heat. Gently mix the layers before serving.
  11. Serve hot with raita and lemon wedges on the side.
