Fragrant Chicken Biryani

An exquisite layered dish featuring aromatic basmati rice, succulent marinated chicken, sweet caramelized onions, and a blend of traditional spices. This renowned Indian delicacy balances rich flavors with varied textures to create an impressive meal worthy of celebration and special gatherings.

What You'll Need

  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 750g bone-in chicken pieces
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or cooking oil
  • 3 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 4 green chilies, split lengthwise
  • ¼ cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • ¼ cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder (adjust according to preference)
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 star anise
  • A pinch of saffron threads soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water for cooking rice
  • Lemon wedges for garnish
  • Raita (yogurt accompaniment) for serving

Preparation Steps

  1. Combine chicken with yogurt, half the ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Allow to marinate for at least 1 hour, preferably longer for deeper flavor.
  2. In a large heavy-bottomed pot, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee and sauté the sliced onions until they turn golden brown and caramelize. Remove half and reserve for layering later.
  3. Using the same pot with remaining onions, add the whole spices (cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, bay leaves, star anise) and cook for 30 seconds until aromatic.
  4. Incorporate the remaining ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until its raw aroma disappears.
  5. Add the marinated chicken pieces, diced tomatoes, coriander powder, cumin powder, and half of the fresh herbs (mint and coriander). Cook for 10-15 minutes until chicken is partially cooked and oil separates from the spice mixture.
  6. Meanwhile, bring a separate large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the soaked and drained rice, cooking until it reaches 70% doneness (approximately 5-7 minutes). Drain thoroughly.
  7. For the layering process: spread half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture. Sprinkle with half the remaining herbs, some of the reserved fried onions, and half the saffron-infused milk.
  8. Create a final layer with the remaining rice, topped with the rest of the fried onions, herbs, and saffron milk. Pour the remaining ghee over everything.
  9. Cover with a tight-fitting lid (or create a seal using dough around the edges). Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes to allow flavors to meld and rice to finish cooking.
  10. After turning off the heat, let the biryani rest undisturbed for 10 minutes. Gently mix the layers before serving.
  11. Serve hot, accompanied by cooling raita and fresh lemon wedges.
