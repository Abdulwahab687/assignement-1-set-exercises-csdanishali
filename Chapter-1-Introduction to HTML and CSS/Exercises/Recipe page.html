Super Moist Banana Bread

Hey there! This is my absolute favorite banana bread recipe that I've been tweaking for years. It's super forgiving and turns out perfectly every time. The key is using REALLY ripe bananas - I'm talking brown and spotty, almost black. The riper they are, the sweeter and more banana-y your bread will be!

Let those bananas get UGLY before using them! If they're not ready but you want banana bread now, you can speed up ripening by putting them in a paper bag with an apple overnight.

Ingredients You'll Need

  • 3-4 very ripe bananas (about 1½ cups mashed)
  • ⅓ cup melted butter (I sometimes use coconut oil)
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
  • ¾ cup sugar (can reduce to ½ cup if you want it less sweet)
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup chopped walnuts or chocolate chips (optional, but highly recommended!)

Let's Bake!

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 4x8 inch loaf pan. I like to line mine with parchment paper too for easy removal.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, mash those spotty bananas with a fork until smooth-ish. Some lumps are totally fine!
  3. Stir the melted butter into the mashed bananas. I usually just use the same fork.
  4. Mix in the baking soda and salt. Then add the sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla extract. Mix well.
  5. Gradually add the flour, stirring just until incorporated. Don't overmix or your bread will get tough!
  6. Fold in walnuts or chocolate chips if using. Sometimes I do half and half!
  7. Pour the batter into your prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with your spatula.
  8. Bake for about 55-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Start checking at 50 minutes because ovens vary.
  9. Let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes, then remove to a wire rack to cool completely.
  10. Slice when cool (if you can wait that long) and enjoy! It's amazing with a smear of butter or cream cheese.
This bread freezes beautifully! I often double the recipe and freeze the second loaf. Just wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and then foil. Defrost at room temperature when you're ready to enjoy!
